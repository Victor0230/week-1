Reflection of module experience:

This report reflects on my experience developing a website using HTML and CSS, focusing on achieving a harmonious integration of visual design and functional layout. I prioritized creating an interface that is not only aesthetically engaging but also intuitive and user-friendly, ensuring both form and purpose align seamlessly.

How did I find the module?

At the beginning, I was excited to dive into HTML, but I quickly felt overwhelmed by the sheer number of tags. I made countless mistakes, for instance, forgetting to close a {div} or {main} tag, which completely broke the page! But each bug taught me something valuable. With every error and moment of frustration, I grew more comfortable with the basics of building a website. When I moved on to CSS, I realized design is not just about picking colorsâ€”it's about visual hierarchy. I spent hours tweaking margins and padding to achieve the perfect alignment. As a perfectionist, I often couldn't resist adjusting elements that were already working fine, which ironically led to broken layouts or new bugs in the code.

Design Decisions

The project began by analyzing various existing websites to determine the optimal structure and layout. Thanks to my prior experience in UI/UX design, conceptualizing and sketching a unique visual framework was relatively straightforward. Initially, I made a decision that might seem unusual from others perspectives: I started practicing by recreating the Clairebook Zoo website to better understand how programming works and to gain hands-on experience. After I got the necessary knowledge, I immediately started AS1.

For my web portfolio, I didn't want to use a plain background color. I attempted to create NASA-style starry effects or other dynamic visuals, but they proved too complex. In the end, I chose to stick with what I knew. Inspired by practical lessons from my university coursework, I developed an animated gradient background. This CSS makes the background shift colors smoothly. The gradient (deep blues/grays) slides side to side every 15 seconds, looping forever. Simple but modern!
